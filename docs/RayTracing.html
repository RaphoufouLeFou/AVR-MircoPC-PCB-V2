RAY TRACING

Software

The initial version of the raytracer was developped in python by d-002.
Then, it was implemented in C++ by me, and ported to the AVR-MicroPC.
The raytracer uses a raymatching algorithm to trace the ray, and determines the color of eatch pixels.
It support having multiples object, like cubes, spheres, a light , a textured floor, and a colored background.

Hardware

On the AVR-MicroPC V1, the raytraced image was rendered at 128x64, and took a few minutes to render.
On the AVR-MicroPC V2, the image was 256x128, but with the same peromance, so a render was taking 10-15 min
On the current AVR-MicroPC V3, the image is still 256x128, but with a faster clock speed and double bufferd VRAM, and took down the render time to 8-9 mins.

Antialiasing

To ​improve the image quality with such a small resolution, ​antialiasing

Without antialiasing

Very pixelated image, but faster to render​ (~9 mins)

With antialiasing x4

Smoother image, but slower to render (~35 mins)

With antialiasing x8

Even smoother image, but way slower (~2h20)
